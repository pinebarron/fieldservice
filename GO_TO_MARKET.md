# Crewatt Go-To-Market Strategy

## Executive Summary

Crewatt is a field service management platform purpose-built for small solar installation teams (2-5 technicians). At $39-49/month flat pricing, we're positioned significantly below the industry average of $50-100/user/month, targeting an underserved segment of the 12,000+ solar installation companies in North America.

## 3. Target Customer Profile

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Company Profile:**
- 2-5 person installation team
- Residential-focused (some light commercial)
- Regional/local service area
- $500K-$2M annual revenue
- Owner-operator or small management team
- Currently using spreadsheets, texts, or paper

**Pain Points (validated by research):**

| Pain Point | % Citing as Top Challenge | How Crewatt Solves |
|------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Scheduling/dispatch inefficiencies | 38% | Calendar, drag-drop scheduling |
| Photo documentation chaos | High | GPS-verified photos, auto-organization |
| Design-to-execution gap | High | Forms, checklists, job templates |
| Integration headaches | Growing | All-in-one platform |
| Software too expensive | Budget constraint | $39-49/month flat |
| Software too complex | Usability | Mobile-first, simple UI |

Sources: [SunBase](https://www.sunbasedata.com/blog/top-5-challenges-solar-installers-face-and-how-to-overcome-them), [Arrivy Guide](https://www.arrivy.com/blog/the-ultimate-guide-to-solar-project-management-software/)

### Buyer Personas

**Primary: Owner-Operator "Mike"**
- Age 35-50, owns small solar company
- Does installs himself + manages 2-3 techs
- Tech-comfortable but not tech-obsessed
- Cares about: crew accountability, customer trust, not losing money
- Frustrated by: paperwork, photo chaos, "where is my crew?" anxiety

**Secondary: Operations Manager "Sarah"**
- Works for slightly larger shop (5-10 people)
- Manages scheduling, dispatch, customer communication
- Looking to professionalize operations
- Cares about: efficiency, documentation, reducing chaos
- Frustrated by: text message coordination, missing job photos

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Current Pricing

| Plan | Price | Users | Rationale |
|------|-------|-------|-----------|
| Team | $39/month | 1-5 | Entry point for small crews |
| Business | $49/month | 1-10 | Core offering for growing teams |

### Pricing Analysis

**Pros of $39-49/month tiered pricing:**
- Significant undercut vs. $50-100/user competitors
- Removes "per-seat" math anxiety for small teams
- Clear upgrade path as teams grow
- Better unit economics than $30 price point
- Room for promotional discounts (first month free, etc.)

**Considerations:**
- Must deliver clear value to justify vs. spreadsheets/free tools
- Support costs need to stay manageable
- Annual pricing option improves cash flow and retention

### Final Pricing Structure

| Tier | Price | Includes | Target |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Team** | $39/month | 1-5 users, all core features | Small crews, owner-operators |
| **Business** | $49/month | 1-10 users, advanced features, priority support | Growing solar companies |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Unlimited users, custom integrations | Large operations |

**Annual Pricing (2 months free):**
- Team: $390/year (save $78)
- Business: $490/year (save $98)

**Why this structure works:**
- $39 is 60%+ below SiteCapture for comparable team size
- Clear upgrade path as teams grow
- Flat pricing removes per-seat anxiety
- Room for promotional discounts

## 8. Customer Discovery Approaches

### Pre-Launch Validation

**1. Customer Interviews (Target: 15-20)**

Find installers via:
- LinkedIn (search "solar installer owner")
- Local solar company websites
- Solar industry meetups
- Reddit r/solar

Interview questions:
- "Walk me through what happens after you finish a solar install"
- "How do you currently track job photos?"
- "What software do you use for scheduling?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of managing your crew?"
- "How much do you pay for software today?"

**2. Landing Page Test**

- Create simple landing page with value prop
- Drive $200-500 in Google Ads traffic
- Measure: Email signups, demo requests
- Goal: 5%+ conversion to email list

**3. Competitive Analysis Calls**

- Sign up for SiteCapture, Jobber, CompanyCam trials
- Document onboarding experience
- Note pricing objections, feature gaps
- Identify differentiation opportunities

### Post-Launch Feedback Loops

**1. Onboarding Calls**
- Offer 15-min setup call to every new customer
- Learn their specific workflow
- Identify missing features

**2. NPS Surveys**
- Send at Day 7, Day 30, Day 90
- Track score and qualitative feedback
- Target: NPS 40+

**3. Churn Interviews**
- Call every customer who cancels
- Understand why they left
- Ask what would bring them back

**4. Feature Request Tracking**
- Log all requests with customer context
- Prioritize by frequency and ICP fit
- Share roadmap publicly

## 11. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Low awareness / hard to find customers | High | High | SEO investment, community presence, partnerships |
| Price too low to sustain | Medium | High | Monitor unit economics, raise prices if needed |
| SiteCapture/competitors lower prices | Medium | Medium | Differentiate on solar-specific features, not just price |
| Solar market downturn | Medium | High | Expand to adjacent markets (HVAC, electrical) |
| Churn from feature gaps | Medium | Medium | Aggressive customer feedback loop, fast iteration |
| Support burden exceeds revenue | Medium | Medium | Self-serve docs, community forum, careful onboarding |
